Champorado

Ingredients
  • 1 cup glutinous rice (malagkit rice)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder or 2 bars of tablea (Filipino cacao)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup milk (optional, for creaminess)
  • Condensed milk (optional, for serving)
Steps:

  1. Cook the Rice: Rinse the glutinous rice under cold water until the water runs clear. In a pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the rice and cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. Once the rice is soft and sticky (about 15-20 minutes), add more water if needed to reach your desired consistency.

  2. Add the Cocoa: If using cocoa powder, dissolve it in a little water or milk and stir it into the pot. If using tablea (Filipino cacao), chop it into small pieces and add it directly to the pot. Stir well to mix the cocoa into the rice and let it cook for another 5-7 minutes until the rice is fully incorporated with the chocolate flavor.

  3. Sweeten and Season: Add sugar and a pinch of salt. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely. Taste and adjust sweetness if necessary. For a creamier version, stir in 1/2 cup of milk. You can use any type of milk (fresh, evaporated, or condensed).

  4. Serve: Once the champorado reaches a creamy consistency, remove from heat. Serve hot in bowls with a drizzle of condensed milk for added sweetness if desired.

Tocilog

Ingredients:
  • 1 pack of tocino (sweet cured pork)
  • 2 cups cooked white rice (leftover rice works well)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Steps:

  1. Cook the Tocino: Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and cook the tocino according to package instructions. Typically, this involves frying the pieces of tocino in a bit of water until the sugar caramelizes and the pork becomes tender. Set aside once cooked.

  2. Make the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice): In the same pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until golden brown and fragrant. Add the leftover cooked rice to the pan, breaking apart any clumps. Stir-fry for about 3-5 minutes, ensuring the rice gets well-coated with the garlic and oil. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Set aside.

  3. Fry the Eggs: In another pan, heat a little oil and fry the eggs sunny-side up or to your preferred doneness.

  4. Assemble the Tocilog: On a plate, arrange a portion of garlic fried rice (sinangag), add a piece of tocino, and place a fried egg on top or to the side.

  5. Serve: Serve hot with a side of sliced tomatoes or cucumber for freshness (optional).

Ensaymada

Ingredients:
  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm milk (110°F/43°C)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup grated cheese (Edam or cheddar, for topping)
  • 1/4 cup sugar (for topping)
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, melted (for brushing)
Steps:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a small bowl, dissolve 1/4 cup of sugar and the yeast in warm milk. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until the yeast becomes foamy. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Create a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture, eggs, and 1/4 cup warm water. Mix until a dough forms. Add the softened butter to the dough and knead for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.

  2. First Rise: Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl and cover with a clean cloth or plastic wrap. Let it rise in a warm place for about 1-1.5 hours or until it has doubled in size.

  3. Shape the Ensaymada: Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air. Divide the dough into 10-12 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball and then flatten it into a rectangle. Roll the dough rectangle into a log or cylinder shape, then coil it into a spiral, tucking the ends underneath. Place each shaped ensaymada onto a greased baking tray or cupcake liners.

  4. Second Rise: Let the shaped dough rise again for about 30-45 minutes, until puffed up and slightly expanded.

  5. Bake the Ensaymada: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake the ensaymadas for 15-20 minutes or until they are golden brown and cooked through. Once baked, remove from the oven and brush the tops with melted butter.

  6. Top with Cheese and Sugar: While the ensaymadas are still warm, sprinkle the tops with grated cheese and sugar to finish. The cheese will melt slightly, adding a delicious topping to the sweet bread.

  7. Serve: Allow the ensaymadas to cool slightly before serving. They can be enjoyed fresh or stored in an airtight container for a few days.

Fried Tuyo and Eggs

Ingredients:
  • 2-3 pieces tuyo (dried salted fish)
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sliced tomatoes or cucumber (optional, for serving)
Steps:

  1. Prepare the Tuyo: Rinse the tuyo briefly under running water to remove excess salt. Pat it dry with paper towels. Heat a pan over medium heat and fry the tuyo for 3-4 minutes per side, or until it becomes crispy and golden brown. Remove from the pan and set aside.

  2. Fry the Eggs: In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed and heat over medium-low heat. Crack the eggs into the pan and fry them sunny-side up or according to your desired doneness. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.

  3. Serve: Plate the fried tuyo and eggs. Optionally, serve with sliced tomatoes or cucumber on the side for freshness.

Adobo

Ingredients:
  • 1 lb chicken or pork, cut into pieces
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorns
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Steps:

  1. Combine soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, bay leaves, peppercorns, and salt in a bowl. Add the chicken or pork and marinate for at least 30 minutes (or up to overnight in the refrigerator).

  2. Heat vegetable oil in a pot over medium heat. Add the meat and sear it until browned on all sides. Add the marinade mixture and water. Bring it to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes or until the meat is tender. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper to taste. Serve with steamed rice.

Sinigang

Ingredients:
  • 1 lb pork belly or pork ribs, cut into pieces
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 pack (about 1 cup) tamarind paste or fresh tamarind (or 1 packet of sinigang mix)
  • 1-2 long green chili peppers (optional)
  • 1/2 cup radish, sliced
  • 1/2 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach) or other leafy greens
  • 1 liter water
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Steps:

  1. In a large pot, bring the water to a boil and add the pork. Simmer for about 30 minutes or until the meat becomes tender.

  2. Add the onion, tomatoes, and tamarind paste (or sinigang mix). Continue to cook for another 10-15 minutes until the vegetables soften and the sour flavor develops.

  3. Add the radish, eggplant, and chili peppers (if using). Simmer for another 5 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.

  4. Add fish sauce for extra flavor and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Add the kangkong (water spinach) last and cook for 1-2 minutes. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Lechon Kawali

Ingredients:
  • 1 lb pork belly
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon peppercorns
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for boiling
  • Vegetable oil for frying
Steps:

  1. In a large pot, combine pork belly, onion, garlic, bay leaves, peppercorns, and salt. Add enough water to cover the pork. Bring it to a boil and then lower the heat to simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour or until the pork is tender.

  2. Remove the pork from the pot and allow it to cool completely. Once cooled, pat the pork dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture.

  3. Heat vegetable oil in a large frying pan or wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, fry the pork belly until golden brown and crispy, turning it to cook all sides evenly. This usually takes about 10-15 minutes.

  4. Slice the lechon kawali into bite-sized pieces and serve with a side of liver sauce or vinegar dipping sauce. Enjoy with rice.

Pancit Malabon

Ingredients:
  • 1 lb rice noodles (pancit malabon noodles)
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup pork belly, sliced into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup smoked fish (tinapa), flaked
  • 1/2 cup cooked fish balls or squid balls (optional)
  • 1/4 cup annatto oil (or 1 tablespoon annatto powder dissolved in oil)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • 1/4 cup fish sauce
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup hard-boiled eggs, sliced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Lemon or calamansi wedges for serving
Steps:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  2. In a pan, heat annatto oil and sauté the garlic and onion until fragrant. Add the shrimp and pork belly, cooking until the shrimp turns pink and the pork is cooked through.

  3. Add the shrimp paste and fish sauce to the pan, stir, and cook for another 2 minutes. Then add the chicken broth and bring it to a simmer. Allow the sauce to thicken slightly for about 5 minutes.

  4. Add the cooked noodles to the sauce and toss well, ensuring the noodles are evenly coated. Add in the smoked fish and fish balls (if using), and stir to combine.

  5. Transfer the pancit malabon to a serving dish. Garnish with sliced hard-boiled eggs, green onions, parsley, and a squeeze of lemon or calamansi on top. Serve hot.

Kaldereta

Ingredients:
  • 1 lb beef (chuck or stew meat), cut into chunks
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 1/4 cup liver spread (optional)
  • 1/4 cup green olives (optional)
  • 1/4 cup grated cheese (optional)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Steps:

  1. In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Brown the beef chunks in batches, then set aside.

  2. In the same pot, sauté onion and garlic until soft and fragrant.

  3. Add the tomato sauce and beef broth. Stir in the soy sauce, vinegar, and sugar. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.

  4. Return the beef to the pot and simmer for 1-1.5 hours until tender.

  5. Add potatoes, carrots, and bell pepper. Simmer until vegetables are tender.

  6. Stir in liver spread, olives, and cheese (optional). Cook for a few more minutes, then season with salt and pepper.

  7. Serve with rice.

Sinampalokang Manok

Ingredients:
  • 1 whole chicken, cut into parts
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 1-2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 cup young tamarind leaves (or 1 packet of sinigang mix)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1-2 long green chili peppers
  • 1 medium-sized green papaya, peeled and sliced
  • 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • Salt and fish sauce to taste
Steps:

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add chicken, onion, tomatoes, and green chili.

  2. Simmer for 30-40 minutes until the chicken is tender.

  3. Add the young tamarind leaves or sinigang mix. Stir to incorporate.

  4. Add the papaya and string beans. Cook for 10-15 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.

  5. Season with salt and fish sauce to taste.

  6. Serve hot with rice.

Chopsuey

Ingredients:
  • 1 cup chicken breast or pork (sliced into strips)
  • 1/2 cup shrimp (optional)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, sliced into thin rounds
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 cup snow peas
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, chopped
  • 1/2 cup cauliflower florets
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch (dissolved in 1/4 cup water)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Steps:

  1. In a pan or wok, heat oil over medium heat and sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.

  2. Add chicken (or pork) and shrimp, cooking until browned and cooked through.

  3. Add carrots, bell pepper, snow peas, cabbage, and cauliflower. Stir-fry for 3-5 minutes.

  4. Stir in soy sauce, oyster sauce, and the dissolved cornstarch mixture. Let it thicken.

  5. Season with salt and pepper.

  6. Serve with rice.
